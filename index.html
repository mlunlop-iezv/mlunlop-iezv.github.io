TUPAC SHAKUR (1971-1996)
POET. ACTOR. RAPPER. ACTIVIST. REVOLUTIONARY.

Tupac Shakur was a legendary American rapper, actor, and poet. Born in New York City to Black Panther activists, he quickly became a defining voice of 1990s hip-hop.

His music was celebrated for its raw, poetic lyrics that balanced "gangsta" themes with deep social commentary on poverty, racism, and injustice. Albums like Me Against the World and All Eyez On Me made him one of the best-selling artists globally.

Tupac was tragically murdered in 1996 at age 25. His legacy endures as a powerful cultural icon and a profound inspiration for countless artists, cementing him as a symbol of resilience and a voice for the marginalized.
