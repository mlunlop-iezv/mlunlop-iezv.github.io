TUPAC SHAKUR (1971-1996)
POET. ACTOR. RAPPER. ACTIVIST. REVOLUTIONARY.

Tupac Shakur was a legendary American rapper, actor, and poet. Born in New York City to Black Panther activists, he quickly became a defining voice of 1990s hip-hop.

His music was celebrated for its raw, poetic lyrics that balanced "gangsta" themes with deep social commentary on poverty, racism, and injustice. Albums like Me Against the World and All Eyez On Me made him one of the best-selling artists globally.

Tupac was tragically murdered in 1996 at age 25. His legacy endures as a powerful cultural icon and a profound inspiration for countless artists, cementing him as a symbol of resilience and a voice for the marginalized.

The end of Tupac's career was tragically caught up in the East Coast–West Coast hip-hop rivalry. This high-profile feud intensely pitted him, representing Death Row Records (West Coast), against his former friend and rival, The Notorious B.I.G. (Bad Boy Records, East Coast).

Tupac was tragically killed in a drive-by shooting in Las Vegas on September 13, 1996, at just 25. His death, followed closely by The Notorious B.I.G.'s murder six months later, devastatingly ended the rivalry.

Despite this tragic loss, Tupac's legacy endures; he remains a powerful cultural icon and inspiration. His commitment to speaking truth to power and his prolific work cement him as a timeless symbol of resilience and a voice for the marginalized.
